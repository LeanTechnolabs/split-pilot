# Frequently asked questions

## What is Split Pilot?

Split Pilot is a free, account-free shared trip expense tracker. Create a trip, share its private link, record expenses and repayments, and see exactly who owes what.

## Is Split Pilot free?

Yes. Split Pilot is free to use.

## Do I need an account?

No. Split Pilot does not require an account or login.

## How do I invite other participants?

Share the private trip link with the financial participants on the trip — the people who will pay, record expenses, or settle up. You do not need to share the link with every trip member, only with those participating financially.

## Do I need to add every person on the trip?

No. Each participant represents someone who pays, and can stand in for one or more members — such as a family, couple, or paying group. Add the financial participants who will manage or settle shared costs, not necessarily every trip member.

## What types of expense splits are supported?

Expenses can be divided equally, by parts, or by exact amounts.

## Can I record repayments?

Yes. Participants can record repayments when money is paid back within the trip. From the balances panel, you can also use **Record payment →** under a recommended transfer to open the Add Transfer screen with the suggested payment prefilled.

## Does Split Pilot calculate who owes whom?

Yes. Split Pilot calculates participant balances and recommends settlement transfers.

## Can I use different currencies?

Yes. Split Pilot supports multiple currencies.

## Are trips publicly searchable?

Trip pages are excluded from search indexing. That means they are not intended to appear in public search results. It does not mean a trip is inaccessible to someone who already has the private link.

## Is a private trip link the same as a password?

No. A private trip link is a sharing mechanism, not traditional password-based authentication. Treat the link as sensitive and share it only with financial participants who need access to the trip.

## Is the application source code public?

No. This repository contains public product documentation and approved media assets only. The production application’s source code is maintained in a separate private repository and is not distributed through this project.
