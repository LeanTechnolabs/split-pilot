# How it works

Split Pilot helps a group track shared trip expenses through a private trip link. Anyone with access to that link may be able to open the trip, so share it only with financial participants who need to manage or settle expenses.

## 1. Create a trip

Start on [splitpilot.app](https://splitpilot.app) and create a trip for your shared outing, holiday, or temporary shared expenses.

## 2. Add participants

Add the people who will pay or settle up.

In Split Pilot, a **participant** is a paying unit — not necessarily every trip member. One participant can represent a family, a couple, a friend group within the trip, or any other set of people who share costs together.

You do not need to add every person on the trip. Add the financial participants who will record expenses, review balances, and settle.

## 3. Share the private trip link

Each trip has a private link. Share that link with the financial participants you added — the people who need access to record expenses and settle up.

You do not need to share the link with every trip member, only with those who are participating financially. Treat the link as sensitive: anyone with it may be able to open the trip.

## 4. Record expenses

As costs come up — meals, transport, lodging, activities, and other shared items — record them in the trip.

## 5. Choose the appropriate split method

For each expense, choose how it should be divided:

- **Equally** — split the cost evenly among selected participants
- **By parts** — assign relative shares when participants represent groups of different sizes
- **By exact amounts** — enter precise amounts for each participant

## 6. Record repayments

When someone pays another person back, record the repayment so the trip stays up to date.

## 7. Review balances

Split Pilot calculates participant balances so the group can see who is ahead and who still needs to settle.

## 8. Follow suggested settlement transfers

When it is time to settle up, use the recommended settlement transfers to understand a practical way to resolve outstanding balances. Each recommendation can include a **Record payment →** link that opens the Add Transfer screen with the suggested payment prefilled.

## Sharing reminder

A private trip link is not a password in the traditional sense, but it should be treated carefully. Share it only with financial participants who need trip access, and avoid posting it in public places.
