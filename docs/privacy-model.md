# Privacy model

This document describes the confirmed privacy-related principles for Split Pilot. It does not make unsupported claims about encryption, anonymization, regulatory compliance, or data-retention periods.

## Confirmed principles

- No account is required to use Split Pilot.
- Trips are accessed using private trip links.
- Trip pages are excluded from search indexing.
- Users should share trip links only with financial participants who need to manage or settle expenses.
- Public documentation in this repository does not contain private trip information.
- A private trip link should be treated as sensitive.

## Private links are sensitive

A trip link is the primary way financial participants open a trip. If someone else obtains the link, they may be able to access that trip. Share links only with people who need to pay, record expenses, or settle — not necessarily every trip member. Avoid posting links in public channels, search-indexed pages, or other places where unintended people could find them.

## Not indexed is not the same as inaccessible

Two ideas are easy to confuse:

1. **Not being publicly indexed**  
   Trip pages are excluded from search indexing, so they are not intended to show up in ordinary public search results.

2. **Being inaccessible to someone who possesses the link**  
   Exclusion from search indexing does not prevent access by someone who already has the private trip link. Possession of the link may be enough to open the trip.

In short: search engines are not meant to list trip pages, but a shared or leaked link can still grant access.

## What this documentation does not claim

This repository does not claim that Split Pilot is end-to-end encrypted, that private links constitute authentication, or that any specific compliance, anonymization, or retention standard applies. Those topics are omitted here because they are not part of the confirmed public documentation set.
